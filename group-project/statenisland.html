STATEN ISLAND

Staten Island is located in the southern west of the other four boroughs of New York City. It is the southernmost part of the entire New York State, separated from it by New York Bay and from New Jersey by Arthur Kill and Kill Van Kull straits. It is inhabited by about 479,500 residents, which makes Staten Island the least populated borough of New York City despite having the third largest area among the other boroughs. Staten Island can be reached by car via the Verrazano Narrows Bridge from Brooklyn, via one of the three bridges connecting New Jersey: Outerbridge Crossing, Goethals Bridge or Bayonne Bridge, or by the Staten Island Ferry from Manhattan. The island history is full of events that go all way back to the seventeenth century when it was purchased by the Mayor of Amsterdam, Michael Pauw, to be transferred to the British a few decades later. Because of the numerous parks it has, Staten Island is known as the borough of parks.
